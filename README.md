## Project Reflection

### How do I approach designing software?

I approach designing software by breaking the project into smaller parts and focusing on one problem at a time. For this project, I first thought about the overall scene I wanted to create and then looked at which objects would need to be built, how they should be placed, and what details would make the scene look complete. That helped me stay organized and gave me a clearer plan before making changes in the code.

### What new design skills has your work on the project helped you to craft?

This project helped me get better at planning a scene visually and thinking about how separate objects need to work together in one space. It also helped me improve my ability to use scale, position, and rotation more carefully so the final scene looked more balanced and realistic. I also had to think more about the way lighting and textures affect the overall look of a project.

### What design process did you follow for your project work?

My design process was gradual. I started with the main objects in the scene and focused on getting their shapes and placement correct first. After that, I made adjustments to proportions, camera movement, textures, and lighting. I kept comparing what I had on screen to what I wanted the finished scene to look like, then made small changes until it came together better.

### How could tactics from your design approach be applied in future work?

The biggest tactic I would take forward is breaking larger work into smaller parts. That made the project feel simpler to manage and made it easier to catch mistakes early. I also think the habit of making small visual adjustments and testing often will help in future programming projects because it keeps problems from piling up.

### How do I approach developing programs?

I approach development by building step by step and testing as I go. I do better when I make one change, check the result, and then move to the next section. That method assisted in this project because graphics work is very visual, so even a small code change could noticeably affect the scene.

### What new development strategies did you use while working on your 3D scene?

One strategy I used more in this project was making repeated small adjustments to object transforms, camera settings, textures, and lighting until the scene looked right. I also spent more time paying attention to how different parts of the code affected the final result on screen. That helped me better comprehend the relationship between the code and the visual output.

### How did iteration factor into your development?

Iteration was a major part of this project. Very little was perfect on the first try. I had to keep adjusting object size, position, lighting strength, camera movement, and other details throughout the milestones. Each version got closer to the final result, and that process helped me improve the overall quality of the scene.

### How has your approach to developing code evolved throughout the milestones, which led you to the project’s completion?

At the beginning, I was more focused on just getting objects to appear correctly. As the project continued, I became more comfortable making finer adjustments and thinking about how the full scene worked together. By the end, I had a better understanding of how to use the existing code structure, apply visual improvements, and debug problems without changing too much at once.

### How can computer science help me in reaching my goals?

Computer science helps me build practical skills that I can apply in different areas of technology and software development. It strengthens my problem solving, logic, and technical confidence. Those skills are important for the kind of work I want to do in the future, especially while I continue building projects for my portfolio and look for opportunities in software and related technical fields.

### How do computational graphics and visualizations give you new knowledge and skills that can be applied in your future educational pathway?

Computational graphics and visualization helped me better understand how math, programming, and design work together. It gave me more experience with coordinates, transformations, structure, and problem solving in a way that is more visual than some other programming projects. That can help me in future classes because it improved both my understanding and my patience with debugging more complex systems.

### How do computational graphics and visualizations give you new knowledge and skills that can be applied in your future professional pathway?

This course helped me build skills in visual problem-solving, iterative development, and working with new code structures. It also gave me experience making improvements based on testing and observation instead of just writing code once and moving on. Those are useful skills in professional work because development commonly involves adjusting, refining, and improving a product over time rather than getting everything right on the first attempt.
